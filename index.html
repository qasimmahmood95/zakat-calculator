Nisab basis
Gram convention
Scholarly difference The nisab is the minimum wealth at which zakat becomes due: 87.48 g of gold or 612.36 g of silver on the default convention (4.374 g per mithqal). Some institutions convert the classical measures as 85 g / 595 g instead — a difference in gram conversion, selectable above; both are in respectable use. For mixed wealth (cash, crypto, business assets), many scholars advise the silver basis: it is the lower threshold, so zakat becomes due sooner — seen as more cautious for the payer and more beneficial to recipients. Others prefer the gold basis as historically more stable in value. Both are computed above; the choice is yours. Commonly held view — consult a scholar for your madhhab.

Your zakat date (hawl)

How do you time your zakat year?

⚠️ A Gregorian year is ~11 days longer than the lunar year, so a fixed Gregorian date means slightly fewer zakat payments over a lifetime. The 2.577% rate (2.5% × 365.25 ÷ 354.37) is an approximation used by some institutions — tracking a Hijri date is preferable.

Zakat falls due once wealth at or above the nisab has been held for one lunar year (hawl) ≈ 354 days — about 11 days shorter than the Gregorian year. Hijri dates shown here use the Umm al-Qura calculated calendar; actual moon sighting in your locality may differ by a day or two. Scholarly difference The approach applied here assesses whatever you hold on your zakat date, even if balances fluctuated during the year (provided nisab was held at both ends of the year); some schools hold that dropping below nisab mid-year restarts the hawl. Commonly held view — consult a scholar for your madhhab.

1 · Cash & bank balances

Current and savings accounts, cash in hand, e-money balances. Foreign-currency balances are converted with the rates you entered in Settings.

2 · Gold & silver

Enter by weight and purity (valued at metal content using your prices above), or enter a value directly.

UK gold coins: legal-tender coins such as Sovereigns and Britannias are exempt from Capital Gains Tax — that is a UK tax rule with no bearing on zakat. They remain fully zakatable at their gold value.
Scholarly difference Personal-use jewellery: the Hanafi school holds gold and silver zakatable however used; the majority of other schools exempt jewellery in customary personal use. This tool counts whatever you enter — if you follow an exemption view, leave exempt items out. Consult a scholar for your madhhab.

3 · Cryptoassets

Bitcoin and other cryptoassets: quantity × price in GBP.

⚠️ Prices are your responsibility. This tool makes no API calls and never fetches prices. Enter each asset’s GBP market price yourself as at your zakat date and verify it against a source you trust.
Scholarly difference The commonly held contemporary view treats cryptoassets held as investments as zakatable wealth at full market value. Staking, lending and token classifications raise further questions — consult a scholar for your madhhab.

4 · Business assets (limited company)

Add the company’s zakatable current assets — cash, trade receivables, stock-in-trade — and its short-term liabilities, each scaled by your ownership %.

Scholarly difference A limited company is a separate legal person, but your zakat obligation is personal: the approach applied here (commonly held for owner-managed companies) is your ownership % × (cash + receivables + stock − short-term liabilities). Fixed assets — equipment, vehicles, premises — are not zakatable. Scholars differ on valuing shareholdings this way versus at market value (especially for passive/listed holdings, which this section is not designed for), and on doubtful receivables (commonly: zakat on good debts annually; on doubtful debts when actually recovered). Consult a scholar for your madhhab.

5 · Investment property

Rental income still held at your zakat date is zakatable like any other cash — take care not to double-count rent already included in a bank balance in section 1. The property itself is generally not zakatable when held to generate income; property bought with the intention of resale is trade stock, zakatable at market value. Scholarly difference Where the intention was mixed or changed after purchase, scholars differ on if and when the property becomes trade stock. Commonly held view — consult a scholar for your madhhab.

6 · Debts & deductions

Money you owe. Choose the type carefully — it decides whether the amount is deducted.

Scholarly difference Position applied (commonly held view): liabilities due within the coming year — bills, tax due, credit cards, and the next 12 months of instalments on long-term debt such as a mortgage — are deductible. The remaining long-term balance is not deducted, since it is not a present claim on your wealth. Other position: some scholars allow deducting the full outstanding balance of long-term debt; this tool shows that figure in the breakdown but does not deduct it. Consult a scholar for your madhhab.

Methodology & privacy

  • Every rule involving scholarly difference is flagged Scholarly difference with the applied position stated as a commonly held view — no named-scholar attributions, no fabricated citations.
  • All arithmetic lives in calc.js, unit-tested with plain Node — see the README for the positions taken and why.
  • Privacy: no accounts, no analytics, no cookies — and the page makes no network requests at all. Your figures are autosaved to your browser’s local storage only (clear them with the button in the summary panel), and can be exported to / imported from a JSON file that never leaves your device.
